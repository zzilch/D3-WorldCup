In the beginning, the countries participating in the World Cup only came from North America, South America and Europe. With the progress of globalization, more countries from different continents have also joined the participating countries in the World Cup.
Before 1982 Spain World Cup, there were no more than 16 participating countries. After that, the number increased to 24 until 1998 France World Cup when it became 32. Furthermore, FIFA officials announced that the 2026 World Cup will be expanded to 48 teams.
Attandance:
At the same time, more and more people came to watch the World Cup. In the early years, due to the inconvenience of transportation, most of the audiences were fans near the host country. With the development of industrialization, international traffic is more developed. The World Cup has gradually become a feast for fans around the world.
It is worth noting that the 1942 and 1946 World Cups were cancelled due to the Second World War.

Historical Data:
From the radar chart on the left we can see the shape of the historical data of the selected country. In general, the upper part of the stars of a strong country has a large area, while the lower part has a small area.
Historical VS:
From the heatmap on the right we can see the historical matchups of the selected countries. Block[A][B] indicates how many times Country A has defeated Country B. The more times the country wins, the hotter the block colors are. Strong countries see more hot blocks from the row, and fewer hot blocks from the colum.
